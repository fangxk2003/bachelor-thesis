\begin{abstract}[zh]
  前交叉韧带（Anterior Cruciate Ligament, ACL）孤立性撕裂与多发韧带膝关节损伤（Multi-Ligament Knee Injury, MLKI）是创伤后骨关节炎的重要危险因素。磁共振成像（Magnetic Resonance Imaging, MRI）可用于骨、软骨及骨髓病变（Bone Marrow Lesion, BML）相关指标的三维定量评估，但人工标注成本高，跨序列域偏移会限制监督模型在临床快速自旋回波（Fast Spin Echo, FSE）数据中的泛化能力。
  \par 本研究建立了基于无监督域适应（Unsupervised Domain Adaptation, UDA）的膝关节MRI分割与量化流程。分割部分采用改进型域对抗神经网络（Domain-Adversarial Neural Network, DANN），结合实例归一化、空洞空间金字塔池化和动态冻结策略处理无标签FSE数据上的跨序列特征迁移问题；临床分析部分开发软骨厚度、胫股关节影像近接触面积和BML候选高信号体积的自动量化工具，并对质控后 \num{579} 例患者进行统计分析。
  \par 改进型DANN在小规模人工标注目标域测试集上获得了可用于后续量化的骨与软骨分割结果。临床统计显示，MLKI组较孤立ACL组胫股关节影像近接触面积更低、BML候选高信号体积更高（$p<0.001$），提示两类损伤在关节空间关系和骨髓水肿负荷上存在定量差异。
  \par 本研究流程可在减少目标域人工标注依赖的条件下，为ACL与MLKI损伤后的影像量化比较提供参考。需要说明的是，本研究中人工验证的分割对象为骨与软骨；BML仅定位为FSE高信号候选区域的自动检测、粗分割与体积量化，并非经精细病灶金标准验证的临床BML分割模型。
\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract}[en]
  Isolated anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) tears and multi-ligament knee injuries (MLKI) are important risk factors for post-traumatic osteoarthritis. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) enables three-dimensional quantitative assessment of bone, cartilage, and bone marrow lesion (BML)-related features, but manual annotation is expensive and cross-sequence domain shift limits the generalization of supervised models on clinical fast spin echo (FSE) data.
  \par This thesis develops a knee MRI segmentation and quantification workflow based on unsupervised domain adaptation. An improved Domain-Adversarial Neural Network (DANN) is used with Instance Normalization, Atrous Spatial Pyramid Pooling, and a dynamic freezing strategy to address cross-sequence feature transfer on unlabeled FSE data. Automated tools for cartilage thickness, image-based tibiofemoral near-contact area, and candidate BML high-signal volume are then applied to \num{579} quality-controlled patients.
  \par The improved DANN obtained bone and cartilage segmentations suitable for downstream quantification on a small manually annotated target-domain test set. The MLKI group showed a lower image-based tibiofemoral near-contact area and a higher candidate BML high-signal volume than the isolated ACL group ($p<0.001$), suggesting quantitative differences in joint spatial relationships and bone marrow edema burden between the two injury patterns.
  \par The proposed workflow supports quantitative comparison between ACL tears and MLKI while reducing dependence on target-domain manual annotation. The manually validated segmentation targets are bone and cartilage; the BML component is limited to automated detection, coarse segmentation, and volume quantification of high-signal candidate regions on FSE images, rather than a clinically validated fine BML lesion segmentation model.
\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract}[zh]
  前交叉韧带（Anterior Cruciate Ligament, ACL）孤立性撕裂与多发韧带膝关节损伤（Multi-Ligament Knee Injury, MLKI）是诱发创伤后骨关节炎（Post-traumatic Osteoarthritis, PTOA）的核心危险因素。
  利用磁共振成像（Magnetic Resonance Imaging, MRI）对软骨及骨髓病变（Bone Marrow Lesions, BML）进行三维定量评估，在早期诊断与预后分析中具有重大临床价值。
  然而，目前的分析面临严重痛点：全像素手动标注极度耗时，且不同扫描序列间的域偏移导致传统监督模型泛化性能崩溃。
  针对上述问题，本文开发了一套无监督/极少监督的深度学习膝关节MRI分割与三维量化分析管线。
  \par 在分割层面，提出了一种改进的域对抗神经网络（Domain Adversarial Neural Network, DANN）。
  通过引入实例归一化（Instance Normalization, IN）滤除对比度风格差异，结合空洞空间金字塔池化（Atrous Spatial Pyramid Pooling, ASPP）增强微小软骨的全局感知，并创新性设计了动态冻结对抗训练策略，在无标签快速自旋回波（Fast Spin Echo, FSE）数据上实现了高精度的跨序列特征迁移。
  在重建层面，结合刚性配准与深度图像先验（Deep Image Prior, DIP）算法，实现了多视图数据的0.5mm各向同性高保真融合。
  \par 在临床应用层面，开发了自动形态学量化工具，并对近600例患者进行了统计学分析。
  结果揭示：相比于孤立ACL撕裂的局灶性高压撞击，MLKI患者的胫股关节接触面积显著锐减、软骨厚度损耗较轻，但BML体积呈极显著的弥漫性扩张（p<0.001）。
  该发现从客观数据层面强有力地验证了高能创伤的“能量耗散”假说与“软骨保护”效应，为创伤机制解析提供了全新的AI方法学范式。
\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract}[en]
  Isolated tears of the anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) and multi-ligament knee injuries (MLKI) are critical risk factors for post-traumatic osteoarthritis (PTOA).
  Three-dimensional quantitative assessment of cartilage and bone marrow lesions (BML) using magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) holds significant clinical value for early diagnosis and prognosis.
  However, full-pixel manual annotation is highly time-consuming, and domain shift between scanning sequences degrades the generalization of supervised models.
  To address these limitations, this thesis proposes an unsupervised/few-shot deep learning pipeline for knee MRI segmentation and 3D quantitative analysis.
  \par For segmentation, an improved Domain Adversarial Neural Network (DANN) is proposed.
  By integrating Instance Normalization (IN) and Atrous Spatial Pyramid Pooling (ASPP), along with a novel dynamic freezing adversarial training strategy, the model achieves high-precision cross-sequence feature migration on unlabeled fast spin echo (FSE) data.
  For reconstruction, combining rigid registration with the Deep Image Prior (DIP) algorithm enables 0.5mm isotropic high-fidelity fusion of multi-view data without high-resolution labels.
  \par Clinically, automated morphological quantification tools were created to analyze nearly 600 patients. The results revealed that compared to the focal impact of isolated ACL tears, MLKI patients exhibited a significantly reduced joint contact area and milder cartilage loss, but a highly significant diffuse expansion in BML volume (p<0.001).
  These findings robustly validate the "energy dissipation" hypothesis and cartilage protection effect in high-energy trauma, establishing a novel AI paradigm for orthopedic imaging analysis.
\end{abstract}

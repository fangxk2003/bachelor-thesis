\begin{abstract}[zh]
  前交叉韧带（ACL）孤立性撕裂与多发韧带膝关节损伤（MLKI）是创伤后骨关节炎的重要危险因素。磁共振成像（MRI）可用于骨、软骨及骨髓病变（BML）相关指标的三维定量评估，但人工标注成本高，跨序列域偏移限制监督模型在临床快速自旋回波（FSE）数据中的泛化能力。本文构建基于无监督域适应训练的膝关节MRI分割与量化管线，并以小规模人工标注目标域测试集验证。
  \par 方法上，本文设计改进型域对抗神经网络（DANN），结合实例归一化、空洞空间金字塔池化和动态冻结策略，探索无标签FSE数据上的跨序列特征迁移。临床应用中，本文开发软骨厚度、胫股关节影像近接触面积和BML候选高信号体积的自动量化工具，对质控后 \num{579} 例患者进行统计分析。结果显示，MLKI组较孤立ACL组胫股关节影像近接触面积更低、BML候选高信号体积更高（p<0.001），提示两类损伤在关节空间关系和骨髓水肿负荷上存在定量差异。需说明的是，本文经人工验证的分割对象为骨与软骨；BML仅定位为FSE高信号候选区域的自动检测、粗分割与体积量化，并非经精细病灶金标准验证的临床BML分割模型。
\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract}[en]
  Isolated anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) tears and multi-ligament knee injuries (MLKI) are important risk factors for post-traumatic osteoarthritis. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) enables three-dimensional quantitative assessment of bone, cartilage, and bone marrow lesion (BML)-related features, but manual annotation is expensive and cross-sequence domain shift limits the generalization of supervised models on clinical fast spin echo (FSE) data. This thesis develops a knee MRI segmentation and quantification pipeline based on unsupervised domain adaptation training and validates it with a small manually annotated target-domain test set.
  \par Methodologically, an improved Domain Adversarial Neural Network (DANN) is created by incorporating Instance Normalization, Atrous Spatial Pyramid Pooling, and a dynamic freezing strategy to explore cross-sequence feature transfer on unlabeled FSE data. Clinically, automated tools are developed for cartilage thickness, image-based tibiofemoral near-contact area, and candidate BML high-signal volume, and are applied to \num{579} quality-controlled patients. The MLKI group shows a lower image-based tibiofemoral near-contact area and a higher candidate BML high-signal volume than the isolated ACL group (p<0.001), suggesting quantitative differences in joint spatial relationships and bone marrow edema burden between the two injury patterns. It should be clarified that the manually validated segmentation targets are bone and cartilage; the BML component is limited to automated detection, coarse segmentation, and volume quantification of high-signal candidate regions on FSE images, rather than a clinically validated fine BML lesion segmentation model.
\end{abstract}
